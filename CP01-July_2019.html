Gato Roboto

Doinksoft

Switch/PS4

For a hot second I honestly thought I was the only person to think up “meowtroidvania” and that it wouldn’t matter what I wrote cos people wouldn’t be able to read about how seriously fantastic this game is through the fits of jealous laughter. I wasn’t, obviously. This game kicks absolute frog-ass. It feels great, the soundtrack and sound design is pitch perfect and the amount of personality and charm the art team squeezes out of so little is jaw dropping. Fur-deration Force. — S

RIYL — Hollow Knight, Downwell, Kero Blaster

Observation

No Code

PC/PS4

Something has gone awfully wrong on this space station and it is up to you to figure out what happened. But here's the thing, you are the space station. The space station's AI to be exact. You play this sci-fi thriller by interacting with computer screens and tapping into security cameras, solving puzzles similar to what you might encounter in a point-and-click adventure game. Observation really nails this constant feeling of unease, feeding the suspicion that maybe you had something to do with what happened. — J

RIYL — Firewatch, being trapped in space, unreliable narrators

ALSO ... Observation and rogue AIs

Ordia

Loju

iOS/Android

A masterclass in simplicity this one; “one-finger fling” your bright little single-celled blob through beautiful (and reasonably checkpointed) vertical environments while dodging obstacles and aceing super satisfying timing challenges that had me feeling like a Celeste speed-runner after I beat more than one in a row. And, as with all good mobile games, the levels take about as much time to beat as your dinner takes to cook/the time until the next train/this unskippable ad break to finish. — S

RIYL — Desert Golf, Aalto’s Adventure, the first bit in Spore when you only have an eye

ALSO ... James’ review on Touch Arcade

P1 Select

Michael Brough

iOS/PC

Admittedly I might just be looking in the wrong places but it’s pretty rare to find a game that is actually properly weird. Even rarer to find something properly weird that is a... game-game, with points (pontos!) rather than an abstract experience/experiment. Although it took a few goes of wading through weird to figure it out, P1 Select is a real game-game, a kind of loose turn-based strategy roguelike with a brilliant core mechanic that looks like what you’d see a garbage-alien playing in an early 00s Cartoon Network cartoon. It kicks butt. — S

RIYL — Ed Edd & Eddy, alien checkers, slime
